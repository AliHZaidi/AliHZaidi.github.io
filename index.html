Hi, I'm Ali! I am a Ph.D. Candidate at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign. I am co-advised by Karrie Karahalios and Ranjitha Kumar. I am interested in human-algorithmic interaction, specifically investigating how to effectively integrate automated assistance into established workflows related to decision making. Currently, I am investigating how to integrate machine-learning based decision support tools into clinical workflows for concussion detection.

Projects

Current Projects
  • Flightpath/NeuroDNA: Building clinician interfaces and evaluating both clinician and patient acceptance of deep learning models for objective measures of mild traumatic brain injury using AR/VR.
  • Building Reliable Home Management Systems of the Future: Synthesizing design principles from qualitative analysis of user interactions with Smart IoT devices in the home and building/evaluating systems built with these design principles.
Past Projects
  • App-Based Task Shortcuts for Virtual Assistants: Using Machine Learning and Natural Language Processing to train mobile voice assistants in mapping task shortcuts to relevant application UI screens.
  • Evaluating Smart Home Resolution Approaches to Routine Conflicts: Investigating the usability of Smart IoT devices for the home integrated with distributed systems principles for routine conflict resolution.
